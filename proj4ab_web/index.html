1. Recovering a Homography

We begin by explaining how we estimate the homography transformation matrix HHH﻿ to map AAA﻿ into the geometry of BBB﻿.

Two images AAA﻿ and BBB﻿ with the same center of projection are related by a homography. To estimate this transform, we first need to label a set of N≥4N\geq 4N≥4﻿ feature correspondence points (xj,yj)↔(xj′,yj′)(x_j, y_j) \leftrightarrow (x_j^\prime, y_j^\prime)(xj​,yj​)↔(xj′​,yj′​)﻿ between both images.

Note that NNN﻿ must be at least 4 because homographies have 8 degrees of freedom due to scale invariance andeach pair of correspondences provides two constraints on HHH﻿.

1b. Estimating the Homography

While a minimum of 4 correspondence pairs are required to solve for HHH﻿, this system is very sensitive to noise. 2. Warping Images with a Homography

In all following sections, we’ll use the term “canvas” to refer to an xyxyxy﻿-axis aligned bounding box that is large enough to contain all points in the image of the homography transform.

2a. Warping One Image

With the homography HHH﻿ relating AAA﻿ and BBB﻿, we map AAA﻿ into BBB﻿ with an inverse warping procedure:

  1. Determine the minimum dimensions of the canvas by warping the corners of AAA﻿ into BBB﻿.
  1. For every canvas pixel (i′,j′)(i^\prime, j^\prime)(i′,j′)﻿ in the quadrilateral formed from the warped corners of AAA﻿, compute the point (x,y)(x,y)(x,y)﻿ in the geometry of AAA﻿ by inverting the homography.
  1. In general, (x,y)(x,y)(x,y)﻿ will not lie exactly on a grid point in AAA﻿, so we interpolate the RGBA color vector C(i′,j′)C(i^\prime, j^\prime)C(i′,j′)﻿ at (x,y)(x,y)(x,y)﻿ from the grid points of AAA﻿. Our code uses scipy.interpolate.RegularGridInterpolator to do this.
  1. After completing steps 2 and 3, we have the color value C(i′,j′)C(i^\prime, j^\prime)C(i′,j′)﻿ at each canvas point (i′,j′)(i^\prime, j^\prime)(i′,j′)﻿, completing the process of inverse-warping AAA﻿ to the canvas with BBB﻿, producing A′A^\primeA′﻿ in the geometry of BBB﻿.
  1. To avoid edge-artifacts, we use a 2-level laplacian pyramid to smoothly blend A′A^\primeA′﻿ and BBB﻿.
    1. We found that a mask created from the Euclidean distance transform on A′A^\primeA′﻿ and BBB﻿ works very well. Our code uses scipy.ndimage.distance_transform_edt to do this.

2b. Image Rectification

An application of homography warping is undoing (rectifying) the perspective distortion of rectangular objects in images. This is theoretically possible because two images of a planar rectangular-looking object, like a tile or poster, are related by a homography.

3. Creating Panoramas

We can extend our one-image warping procedure to create panoramas. Since two images with the same center of projection are related by a homography, we can form a panorama by warping a set of images with the same center of projection into a particular image in this set. This can be implemented by recursively applying the the one-image warp to grow a panorama one image at a time.
