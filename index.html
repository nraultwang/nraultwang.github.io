Nicolas Rault-Wang

BA double major in Applied Math & CS, UC Berkeley ('25) • Post-Bac Researcher, Caltech (Jan 2026 - present) • Incoming PhD, MIT EECS (Fall 2026)

I design and build the computational infrastructure that powers next-generation scientific instruments. My research focuses on the end-to-end pipeline of physical discovery: from physical-layer distributed systems and high-throughput data engines to computational imaging and physics-informed ML models.

An underlying theme of my work—developed across projects at Berkeley's Space Sciences Laboratory and Caltech—is the translation of raw, uncalibrated sensor streams into real-time scientific insight. Whether optimizing distributed arrays for optical SETI or modeling complex physical signals, I focus on the systems and algorithms that bridge bare metal to deep learning.
